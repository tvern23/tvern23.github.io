Is public data sufficient?

Hurley-Walker et al. (2016) published the first survey results from GLEAM Year 1, in the form of an extragalactic catalogue, based on images processed with a Briggs' weighting scheme, with the robustness parameter set to -1, favouring resolution over sensitivity and large-scale structure detection. If your work requires only catalogue or image data from this publication, then you do not need to use any GLEAM Builders' lists, and indeed filling in this form is optional (although appreciated, so it's clear who is working on what). There are also other public MWA datasets available, such as the source measurements in the deep EoR0 field (Offringa (2016) & Carroll (2016)) a catalogue of HII regions (Hindson et al. (2015)), and so forth. Check the mwatelescope.org website for a list of current publications to see if your work can be performed using a public dataset. We trust that if you have already proposed a project and have been working on it before the data became public, you continue to abide by the GEG policies and will include any applicable Builders' Lists on resulting publications.
